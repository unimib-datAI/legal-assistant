Query 5: What is a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) and what should it contain?

RAG Response:
The retrieved content provides limited detail on Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs). Based on general GDPR principles, a Transfer Impact Assessment is a process that organizations must undertake to assess the risks associated with transferring personal data to a third country or an international organization outside the European Economic Area (EEA) where an adequacy decision by the European Commission is not in place.

### Core Legal Requirement:
- **GDPR Article 35** outlines the requirement for a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) in specific cases, including when transferring personal data to a third country or an international organization.
- **GDPR Article 36** mandates that the controller consult the supervisory authority if the DPIA indicates a high risk in the absence of mitigating measures.
- **GDPR Article 37** requires the appointment of a Data Protection Officer (DPO) who should be consulted when carrying out a DPIA.

### Practical Implementation:
1. **Identify Data Transfers**: Determine which personal data is being transferred, the purpose of the transfer, and the recipient's location.
2. **Assess Risks**: Evaluate the potential risks to data subjects' rights and freedoms in the absence of adequate safeguards.
3. **Mitigation Measures**: Implement appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs), or obtaining explicit consent.
4. **Consult DPO**: Seek advice from the DPO on conducting the TIA and ensuring compliance with GDPR requirements.
5. **Document Findings**: Maintain records of the TIA process, including identified risks, mitigation measures, and decisions taken.

### Key Considerations:
- **Risk Assessment**: Consider the nature, scope, context, and purposes of the data transfer to determine the level of risk.
- **Safeguards**: Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data protection during the transfer.
- **Data Subject Rights**: Ensure that data subjects' rights are respected and protected during and after the transfer process.
- **Supervisory Authority Notification**: Inform the relevant supervisory authority if the TIA reveals a high risk that cannot be mitigated.

### Documentation & Accountability:
- **Records**: Maintain documentation of the TIA process, including risk assessments, mitigation measures, and decisions made.
- **Audit Trails**: Keep a record of the steps taken to comply with GDPR requirements regarding data transfers.
- **Policies**: Establish clear policies and procedures for conducting TIAs and ensuring data protection during transfers.

### Related Obligations:
- **GDPR Article 44-50**: Provide additional guidance on transferring personal data to third countries or international organizations.
- **GDPR Article 58**: Empowers supervisory authorities to monitor compliance with GDPR requirements, including data transfers.

For definitive guidance on Transfer Impact Assessments, consult the full text of GDPR Articles 35, 36, and 37, and official guidance from the relevant supervisory authority.

Expert response:
A TIA evaluates whether, for a given transfer of personal data to a third country on the basis of the SCCs or other safeguards, the laws and practices in the destination country may impinge on the effectiveness of the transfer tool. The TIA should document: (1) the transfer details (parties, roles, data categories, purposes, recipients, onward transfers); (2) the transfer tool used (e.g., SCCs module); (3) an assessment of the third country’s legal landscape, particularly with respect to government access to data and redress; (4) the likelihood of problematic access to personal data in practice; (5) technical and organisational measures (e.g., strong encryption with key control in the EEA, split processing, transparency); and (6) conclusions and residual risks, including whether to proceed with the proposed transfer or adopt additional measures. Evidence and sources relied upon in the TIA should be maintained and the TIA should be re-assessed periodically or upon any material change which may impact the transfer. TIAs should be integrated with vendor due diligence and contract management.
